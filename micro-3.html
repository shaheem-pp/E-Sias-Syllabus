BSc. MB

SEMESTER 3


Environmental and Sanitation Microbiology

MODULE I

Microbiology of air - atmospheric layers, organisms in air, distribution and sources. Disease forecasting in plants. Indoor and outdoor air. Droplet nuclei, aerosol, infectious dust. Microbiological sampling of air - gravity slide, plate exposure, vertical cylinder, Hirst spore trap, Rotorod sampler, Andersen sampler, hand held air sampler, impingers and filtration. Advantages and disadvantages of these techniques. Brief account of air borne transmission of harmful microbes and air borne infections.

MODULE II

Aquatic Microbiology: Aquatic environment, distribution of microorganisms in aquatic environment - fresh water, estuarine and marine water systems. Factors influencing growth and distributions. Water Purification procedures for single dwelling and municipal water supplies, Concept of indicator organisms, Microbiological examination of water. BOD, COD, Waste water treatment steps and methods. Eutrophication and algal bloom. Brief account of water borne diseases and transmission.

MODULE III

Solid waste management: Sources and types of solid waste, need for management, Landfills, composting, vermi- composting, anaerobic digesters, methanogenesis and production of biogas. Design and management of biogas plant.

MODULE IV

Xenobiotic metabolism - Novel pollutants, persistance and biomagnification, Recalcitrant halocarbons- nitroaromatic compounds, PCB, alkyl benzyl sulphonates, and petroleum hydrocarbons - their biodegradation. Bioremediation of polluted environment - Oil spills, heavy Metals and other xenobiotics. Microbial leaching and corrosion of metals.
